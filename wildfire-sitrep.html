Predictive Services Discussion

The focus for critical fire weather will shift northeastward today as milder and calmer conditions expand from the northern Intermountain West into the Great Basin and most of the Southwest. Extreme downslope wind gusts as high as 50-80 mph will affect the mountains of Colorado and Wyoming, with high winds likely to extend into the High Plains and downwind of the Black Hills, as well. Relative humidity will generally range from 10-20% where winds are strongest, locally falling into the single digits for the lower elevations. Despite lighter winds farther south and west, dry and breezy conditions will persist, with overall improving weather for fires in southeastern Nevada, Utah, most of the West Slope and Arizona as compared to the last few days. Gusty Cascade Gap winds will continue in the Northwest, while California will see slightly drier air and a decrease in winds for most areas. Hot, dry and windy conditions will focus over the Southern Plains, where fuels are not as receptive as farther west. The rest of the northern Intermountain West will not be quite as cool as yesterday, but temperatures will remain 15-30 degrees below average for late June. Alaska will remain cool and wet with limited fire potential, while the Florida peninsula will see spotty thunderstorms and hot conditions as the Carolinas trend drier.
