Predictive Services Discussion

Elevated to locally critical fire weather conditions are expected near and east of the Cascades and northern Sierra, where gusty west to southwest winds, low relative humidity, and recent lightning activity will support new fire starts and large fire growth. Gusty west winds will also develop across the central Idaho Mountains and much of the Northern Rockies as showers and thunderstorms continue across the Southwest, Great Basin, and into the Northern Rockies, bringing thunderstorm activity to many areas that have recently experienced hot and dry conditions. Farther east, breezy conditions and low relative humidity and hot temperatures will support elevated fire weather conditions across the southern High Plains, where isolated mixed wet and dry thunderstorms may lead to new fire starts. In Minnesota, a weak cold front will bring locally breezy conditions followed by isolated to scattered thunderstorms tonight. Heavy rain and possible flash flooding are also expected across portions of the southern Atlantic Coastal States and Middle Mississippi Valley. Farther south, isolated thunderstorms may reach southern Florida, where dry fuels remain receptive to new ignitions.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Risk areas forecast (valid 241200Z - 251200Z). See discussion below.
As an upper-level trough approaches the Pacific Northwest coast today, a series of disturbances cresting the upper-level ridge over the West will result in increased flow aloft from the Pacific Northwest into the northern Plains. Given the hot and dry airmass in place over much of the region, deep boundary-layer mixing will promote gusty surface winds raising fire-weather concerns in an increasingly favorable fuelscape. ...Northeastern California...Northwestern Nevada...South-Central/Southeastern Oregon... Very hot and dry conditions will result in multiple hours of single-digit RH values across the region. With enhanced southwesterly flow aloft brushing the area, deep vertical mixing will result in west-southwesterly surface winds approaching 20 mph. Given the dry fuels (ERC values exceeding the 80th percentile) and recent lightning events with limited rainfall, critical fire weather conditions are likely. ...Northern Montana... Very strong winds aloft and a deep, well-mixed boundary layer will result in sustained afternoon westerly surface winds approaching 20 mph in the presence of RH values dipping just below 15%. These elevated conditions will cure the fuels for the coming days.
