Valid: Sun Apr 26, 2026 12:00Z – Mon Apr 27, 2026 12:00Z ...EXTREMELY CRITICAL FIRE WEATHER AREA FOR EASTERN NEW MEXICO AND THE FAR WESTERN TEXAS PANHANDLE... ...CRITICAL FIRE WEATHER AREA FOR THE SOUTHERN HIGH PLAINS... Critical to extremely critical fire weather conditions are expected today across portions of the southern High Plains. A low-amplitude upper wave traversing the lower CO River Valley will migrate across the Southwest through the day as an attendant mid-level jet noses into the southern High Plains by peak heating. This will promote steady deepening of a lee cyclone across eastern CO/western KS, resulting in strengthening west/southwest winds across the southern High Plains. Widespread 20–25 mph sustained winds are likely across the region with gusts upwards of 35–45 mph possible. A swath of 30–35 mph winds will likely emerge across eastern NM into far western TX, supporting a corridor of extremely critical fire weather conditions. A dry air mass has been in place across the Southwest/southern High Plains with afternoon RH minimums largely in the single digits to low teens. Although most guidance depicts afternoon RH minimums in the low to mid teens, RH values will most likely fall into the 5–10% range again today. Given preceding days of dry/windy conditions and increasing ERC values (largely near the 85th percentile), receptive fuels will be in place and will support the fire weather concern.
