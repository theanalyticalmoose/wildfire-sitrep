Predictive Services Weather Discussion
A cold upper-level trough will continue to push into the Pacific Northwest and Great Basin Tuesday bringing cooler temperatures to the tune of 10–20 degrees Fahrenheit below normal. Areas of rain and mountain snow showers are expected with wetting amounts across portions of the Pacific Northwest into the Northern Rockies. Dry and breezy to windy conditions with minimum relative humidities in the teens and southwesterly gusts up to 55 mph are likely across the deserts of the Southwest northeastward into the 4 Corners region. Fire Weather Watches are in effect across portions of Southern Utah and southwestern Colorado. More showers and thunderstorms are likely across most of the Rockies, Front Range, Northern Plains and Upper Midwest eastward into parts of Minnesota and Wisconsin. However, a drier area is likely to develop across the Central Plains northwest into Montana. Fire weather headlines are in effect for gusty winds with low relative humidities across part of eastern Montana and western North Dakota. Meanwhile, copious amounts of moisture associated with a stationary front across Texas, the Lower Mississippi, Tennessee, and southern Ohio River valleys eastward into Florida and to the east coast of the Mid-Atlantic will bring more showers and thunderstorms with moderate to heavy rainfall there.

Great Basin
Southwesterly flow aloft and a deepening surface low will promote a strengthening surface pressure gradient across the region. Sustained southwesterly surface winds of 20–30 mph expected. Deeply mixed and dry boundary layer will support RH reductions of 15–20%. Elevated to locally critical fire weather threat amid cured/drying fine fuels, primarily in lower elevation/mid-slope areas.
Elevated
Northeastern Montana and Extreme Western North Dakota
Southeasterly winds will accelerate to 15–20 mph across much of the northern High Plains as a surface trough develops. Limited low-level moisture return will support RH reductions to 15–20% during peak afternoon heating. Delayed green up and existing dry fuels align with these dry and breezy conditions. Afternoon instability may support isolated dry thunderstorm development along far southern and western MT.
