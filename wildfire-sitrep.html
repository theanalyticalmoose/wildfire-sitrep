Predictive Services Discussion

Isolated to scattered wet and dry thunderstorms will develop across Washington and the Northern Rockies today as gusty west to southwest winds strengthen behind the activity over northern California and much of Oregon. Relative humidity will fall to 10–15% across the western Great Basin, southwestern Idaho, and southern Oregon, where temperatures will reach 5–10°F above normal, exceeding 100°F at lower elevations. Extreme heat will also expand across southern California and eastern Arizona as monsoonal thunderstorms shift eastward into the Four Corners, central Rockies, and Great Plains, where heavy rainfall and flash flooding are possible across eastern Colorado and northeastern Kansas. Hot and dry conditions will also prevail from the Northern Plains into the Great Lakes and across much of Texas and the southern Plains, except along the southeast Texas coast, where Tropical Storm Bertha is expected to make landfall this afternoon with sustained winds of 40–55 mph, heavy rain, and thunderstorms, while its moisture continues to support heavy rain and possible flash flooding across portions of the southern Mid-Atlantic, Carolinas, and Tennessee Valley. Farther east, hot, dry, and breezy conditions will persist across portions of the far southeastern United States.
