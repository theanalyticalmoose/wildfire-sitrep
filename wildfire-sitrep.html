Predictive Services Weather Discussion

Widespread elevated to critical conditions will persist across portions of the Southwest, Great Basin, and Rocky Mountain areas, as well as central California valleys today. Across the Southwest, Great Basin, and Rocky Mountain areas, mostly westerly winds 15-25 mph with gusts 30-45 mph and relative humidity values between 5-20% are expected, with the highest winds expected across the Front Range in Colorado and large portions of central Wyoming, where gusts may exceed 50 mph in some areas. Across central California valleys, Las Vegas, and southern portions of the Southwest, temperatures between 95-105 degrees are expected in conjunction with relative humidity values below 15% and wind gusts between 15-30 mph.

In the northern Rockies, widespread rain with high elevation snow will continue to move east across northern Idaho, northern Wyoming, and large portions of Montana accompanied by high relative humidity values and breezy west winds with gusts of up to 40 mph, which will extend into southeastern Idaho where gusts may exceed 50 mph.

In the eastern U.S., scattered showers and thunderstorms will continue to move east across the Midwest and Northeast. Widespread, higher amounts of precipitation are expected across the entire Eastern Area, bringing relative humidity values along the coast to at least 45%. In Alaska, warmer and drier conditions are expected across central Alaska, where relative humidity values below 25% are expected. Scattered light showers will persist across southeastern Alaska today.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Valid June 10, 1200Z – June 11, 1200Z · NWS Storm Prediction Center, Norman OK
CRITICAL — East-Central Wyoming and Far Northwest Nebraska
Strong westerly surface winds in the wake of a cold front beneath pronounced mid-level flow. West winds of 25-30 mph and RH as low as 15% will align with pockets of dry fuels, maintaining a Critical fire weather threat in eastern WY and far northwestern NE.
Includes Casper, Douglas, and Lusk WY; Chadron and Harrison NE areas along the central High Plains.
ELEVATED — Eastern Great Basin, Colorado Plateau & Four Corners
Weaker but still impactful westerly flow will support Elevated fire weather conditions as westerly 10-15 mph winds and RH values of 10-15% overlap dry fuels. Across portions of southeast Utah, a small corridor may experience localized briefly critical winds (west-southwesterly at 15-25 mph) amid widespread warm and dry conditions.
Eastern Utah, western Colorado, and the Four Corners region, including Moab, Grand Junction, and Durango.
ELEVATED — Sacramento Valley
Dry, post-frontal northerly flow funneling into the Sacramento Valley will promote an Elevated fire weather threat to the region and adjacent valley foothills. Sustained northerly winds of 10-15 mph (locally up to 20 mph) and RH at or below 15% coalescing amid receptive fuels. Dry and breezy conditions persist overnight, leading to an extended burning period and continued concerns Thursday.
Sacramento Valley and adjacent foothills, including Redding, Red Bluff, and Chico CA.
