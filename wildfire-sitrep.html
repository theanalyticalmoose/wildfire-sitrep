Predictive Services Discussion
Well above normal temperatures will continue in Minnesota today, while cooler temperatures are expected today in Wisconsin and Michigan, but very dry air with minimum relative humidity of 20–30% will persist. In addition, easterly winds 10–20 mph will create areas of locally elevated conditions. In the Southwest, dry conditions will persist as well, with westerly winds 10–15 mph amid minimum relative humidity of 5–12% and poor overnight recovery for mid-slopes and ridges. An upper-level low will move through the northern Rockies with significant precipitation, heaviest near and east of the Divide in Montana. Scattered showers and thunderstorms will continue in much of the Southeast, while dry northerly winds of 10–15 mph are expected in the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic. Isolated to scattered showers will continue near and south of the Alaska Range with below normal temperatures, but warming and drying will begin in western Alaska and the western Interior.
