🌤️ Predictive Services Weather Discussion
Red Flag Warnings for wind gusts up to 45 mph with low relative humidity in the teens are in effect for Monday across portions of northeast California, southeastern Oregon, and northwest Nevada as a low-pressure trough moves in from the northwest. Further west, west of the Cascades in Washington and Oregon, measurable rainfall with some wetting amounts of a quarter of an inch to an inch are expected. East of the Cascades & across much of the Great Basin and Desert Southwest minimum relative humidities will be mostly in the teens. Across the Rockies isolated to widely scattered mixed wet & dry thunderstorms are likely with the greatest coverage likely to be across northern Idaho and western Montana as well as across portions of central & eastern Arizona into New Mexico. Wetting rains of a quarter of an inch to an inch are likely across much of New Mexico. A swath of showers & thunderstorms is also expected along the Front Range of the Rockies & southern High Plains from New Mexico & Texas north to northeast across Nebraska & adjacent areas. Meanwhile, a slow-moving frontal system extending from the Gulf Coast northeastward into the Northeast is likely to bring widespread quarter of an inch amounts up to as much as 2 inches for most areas from the Southeast northward all the way across northern Maine.
🔴 SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook — Valid May 25–26, 2026 (1200Z–1200Z)
CRITICAL — Northwestern Nevada into Southeastern Oregon
Great Basin Region · Including NW Nevada and SE Oregon
A strengthening surface pressure gradient ahead of an approaching cold front will promote sustained southwesterly surface winds of 20–25 mph, with above-normal temperatures and minimum RH values around 15%. Recent fire activity indicates fuels are receptive to wildfire spread. Deep boundary layer mixing may support occasional gusts to 30–35 mph. This combination of winds, RH, and cured/drying lower-elevation fuels results in a critical fire weather threat this afternoon.
ELEVATED — Adjacent Great Basin Portions
Great Basin Region · Adjacent areas surrounding the Critical zone
Sustained southwesterly surface winds of 15–25 mph expected to overlap reduced RH of 15–25%. Elevated fire weather conditions across adjacent portions of the Great Basin not covered by the Critical area.
DRY THUNDERSTORMS — Southwest (AZ/NM)
Southwest Region · Along/south of the Mogollon Rim and lower elevation areas
A mid/upper trough and plume of mid-level moisture shifting into the Southwest will contribute to isolated-to-scattered thunderstorms over receptive fuels (ERCs around 90th percentile). Initially high-based with dry boundary layers. High-based thunderstorms with lower rainfall totals likely along/south of the Mogollon Rim — isolated Dry Thunderstorm highlights remain for lower elevation fringes. Mixed wet/dry thunderstorms possible with up to 0.5" rainfall in west-central NM into east-central AZ.
