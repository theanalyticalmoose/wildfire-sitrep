Weather Discussion — Predictive Services, NIFC

Very dry conditions will persist across much of the northern Gulf Coast, Mid-Atlantic, and Southeast, with minimum relative humidity of 10–25%. Lighter winds are expected in the Appalachians today, but breezy northeast winds 12–20 mph will develop from South Georgia into much of Florida creating elevated to locally critical conditions. Sea breezes will also develop off the Gulf and Atlantic coasts in the afternoon, which could bring wind shifts to active fires nearer the coasts.

Very low relative humidity of 5–10% and light winds will persist across the Front Range and central High Plains, while elevated relative humidity is expected in the southern Plains but rainfall will be confined to central and east Texas. Strong south to southwesterly winds gusting to 65 mph will develop across portions of Nevada amid low relative humidity of 10–20%, with breezy winds gusting to 40 mph extending into the rest of the Great Basin, northern Rockies, and Arizona.

Widespread rain will continue in much of northern and central California, with snow above 6,000 feet, with showers also spreading into the Northwest and Nevada in the afternoon and overnight. Below normal temperatures are expected across Alaska with some snow showers in the eastern Interior, while drier conditions and trade winds begin to return to Hawai'i.
