Predictive Services Discussion

Scattered showers and wet thunderstorms will continue across the Southwest and eastern Great Basin into much of Colorado, Wyoming, and western Montana. Heavy rainfall and possible flash flooding are possible across portions of the central Rockies. Farther west, isolated thunderstorms, including a mix of wet and dry storms capable of producing gusty outflow winds, are possible this afternoon along the Cascades, east of the Cascades in Oregon and southern Washington, and along the Sierra Nevada. As an offshore low deepens, southwest winds will strengthen across California and the broader Northwest, while very hot and dry conditions persist across much of southern California, the Inland Northwest, Northern Rockies and southern Great Plains, while the Great Lakes and far Northeast experience seasonably mild conditions. A cold front will bring widespread rain to much of the Atlantic Coast, except from the Carolina coast through Florida, with severe thunderstorms possible across portions of the Mid-Atlantic. Meanwhile, Tropical Storm Bertha will continue westward across the Gulf of America, bringing wetting rainfall to the Texas and Louisiana coasts while producing strong winds across portions of the southeastern United States.
