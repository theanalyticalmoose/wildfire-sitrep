Predictive Services Discussion

The NIFC Predictive Services Discussion for this report references external National Weather Service products and links. Key fire weather drivers as reported by NWS and SPC are summarized below.

Broad mid-level troughing is intensifying over the eastern US as a weaker perturbation merges across the northern Gulf Coast. The associated southwesterly flow aloft is strengthening, driving a cold front into the Gulf. High pressure remains over the Great Basin with northwesterly flow persisting over the central US.

In the Southeast, gusty southwesterly winds and dry conditions are driving elevated to locally critical fire weather conditions across Florida ahead of the approaching cold front. After frontal passage this evening, widespread showers and thunderstorms will bring relief.

In the Northern Rockies and High Plains, a surface low tracking from southern Canada into the eastern Dakotas is trailing a cold front that will bring gusty northerly winds and low humidity to parts of MT, WY, and the western Dakotas. Elevated fire weather conditions are likely where dry fuels are present.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook — Valid May 2 1200Z – May 3 1200Z
⚠ Critical Fire Weather Conditions Possible — Florida (Locally)
Affected area: Southern and central Florida Peninsula
A couple hours of locally critical conditions possible through the afternoon where occasional gusts of 15–20 mph could overlap with RH below 35%. Most likely farther south and east where humidity is expected to be lower and less impacted by clouds. While critical conditions are possible, they are unlikely to be widespread or of long duration. Fire-weather concerns end this evening as the cold front moves southward.
⚡ Elevated Fire Weather Conditions — Florida
Affected area: Southern and central Florida Peninsula
Widespread dry and breezy conditions expected ahead of the cold front. Gusty westerly winds with RH below 40% for much of the region where rainfall has been limited. Surface gusts of 10–20 mph possible with mid/upper-level cloud cover reducing but not eliminating diurnal mixing.
⚡ Elevated Fire Weather Conditions — Northern Rockies / High Plains
Affected area: Montana, Wyoming, and the western Dakotas
Strong northerly winds of 15–25 mph (gusts up to 30 mph) expected for several hours following cold frontal passage. Humidity minimums below 30% likely despite variable cloud cover from cooler air mass. Elevated fire weather conditions given dry fuels in the region. Fire Weather Watches in effect for portions of MT and ND.
