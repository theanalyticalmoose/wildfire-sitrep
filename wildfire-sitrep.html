Weather Discussion

Thunderstorms will develop across much of the Intermountain West today as monsoonal moisture aloft quickly surges into the region. This activity will initially be on the drier side, but some wetting rainfall is possible where several rounds occur. New lightning ignitions will be most likely across southern portions of the Great Basin and West Slope, extending into Arizona and northwestern New Mexico, where faster storm motions and parched fuels will align. Erratic outflow winds will likely challenge many of the established wildfires through the afternoon and evening hours, and severe wind gusts over 60 mph will be possible locally. Quick-moving but more isolated thunderstorms will also affect the Northwest into Idaho and western Montana. Hot, dry and unstable conditions will otherwise be the rule throughout the Intermountain West ahead of the more unsettled conditions. Dry air will overspread the Southeast behind a front that will focus widely scattered thunderstorms across the Florida peninsula. Showers and thunderstorms will spread a bit farther north and west in the Alaska Interior, with the warmest temperatures over western Alaska to the North Slope as areas farther south remain milder.

CRITICAL — Scattered Dry Thunderstorms over western & central Utah
An upper-level shortwave trough entering the Great Basin triggers high-based showers and scattered dry thunderstorms, beginning in southern Utah and spreading regionally with daytime heating. Critically dry fuels and sub-cloud dryness create an environment for lightning ignitions and potentially very strong outflow winds. Storms transition from dry to a wet/dry mix over Utah and the Colorado West Slope through the evening; gusty outflows threaten active wildfires, with pyrocumulus development highly possible and pyrocumulonimbus not out of the question.
