Weather Discussion — Predictive Services, NIFC

Critical fire weather conditions will occur in several locations today. The first is over southern Utah into northern Arizona and extreme northwest New Mexico due to the forecast of isolated dry thunderstorms after a prolonged dry period. The other location will occur over the Coastal Carolinas where temperatures in the 90s to near 100°F and relative humidity levels ranging from 30–40% will precede scattered afternoon and evening thunderstorms. Any storms which form could produce erratic wind gusts to 45 mph.

Elsewhere, the main weather of consequence will be a cluster of strong to severe thunderstorms initiating over central Nebraska and Kansas in the morning and spreading south and east into the lower Missouri Valley during the afternoon and evening. Heavy rain and large hail are expected. Farther west the main story will revolve around hot temperatures with readings forecast above 100°F from the interior valleys and southwest corner of California into southern Arizona and southwest New Mexico. The hot temperatures will combine with minimum relative humidity levels in the teens to lower 20s.

Additionally, over the interior of Alaska in the Delta Junction area, winds of 20 to 35 mph with gusts to 50 mph will coincide with warm temperatures and relative humidity values in the 20 to 25 percent range.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook — Valid June 13–14, 2026

Critical — Southern Utah / Northern Arizona / NW New Mexico
Isolated dry thunderstorms following a prolonged dry period. Outflow winds expected gusty and erratic near thunderstorms and areas of virga, driving rapid fire spread in critically dry fuels. Includes Henry Mountains, Color Country, and Grand Staircase areas.
Critical — Coastal Carolinas
Temperatures forecast in the 90s to near 100°F with relative humidity 30–40%. Scattered afternoon and evening thunderstorms; erratic wind gusts to 45 mph could rapidly spread fire in drought-stressed fuels.
Elevated — Interior California / Southern Arizona / SW New Mexico
Dangerously hot temperatures above 100°F across interior valleys of California, southern Arizona, and southwest New Mexico. Minimum relative humidity in the teens to lower 20s with very low fuel moisture.
Elevated — Interior Alaska (Delta Junction)
S winds 20–35 mph gusting to 50 mph combined with warm temperatures and RH 20–25%. Conditions support rapid fire spread in grassy and timber fuels through Sunday.
