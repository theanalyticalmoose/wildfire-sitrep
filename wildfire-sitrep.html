Predictive Services Discussion

Scattered dry thunderstorms will focus a bit farther to the east today, sparking an uptick in new ignitions across much of southern Idaho, northeastern Nevada and far northern Utah. Thunderstorms will be more isolated with westward extent into northern California and southeastern Oregon. Critical fire weather with relative humidity as low as 5-15% and west-southwest wind gusts from 30-45 mph will stretch from western Wyoming into northern parts of the West Slope and much of eastern and southern Utah. Farther to the south, from the Four Corners into western Arizona, a critical burn environment will take shape due to the combination of hot temperatures, relative humidity in the single digits and gusty southwesterly winds. Westerly Cascade gap winds will increase across eastern Washington and Oregon, potentially affecting any of the active fires in the Northwest. Hot and unstable conditions, along with scattered afternoon thunderstorms will continue across the Florida peninsula. Look for severe thunderstorms and areas of significant flash flooding from eastern Colorado through portions of the Missouri Valley.

The potential for lightning-ignited fires will also increase across central and eastern Interior Alaska as thunderstorms become more numerous and temperatures continue to trend higher. Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands will see another hot day, with dry trade winds maintaining an elevated risk for fire activity.
