Predictive Services Discussion

Temperatures 10–25°F above normal will occur across much of the West and extend into the northern Plains. Breezy south winds ahead of a cold front in the northern Plains and northwest winds behind it will gust to 45 mph amid relative humidity of 15–25% creating widespread elevated conditions.

Locally elevated conditions are also likely to the east in portions of western Minnesota due to the breezy southerly winds ahead of the front although relative humidity will not be as low. In the West, relative humidity will fall to 5–15% from the Sierra Front to the central and southern Rockies, west of the Divide, but winds will be light overall.

Scattered showers and thunderstorms will develop near the northern Gulf Coast today but will be less numerous than yesterday. Fewer showers are expected across southern Alaska today, with temperatures near normal. Breezy trade winds are expected across Hawai'i with isolated windward showers.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook — Valid May 11–12, 2026
Critical
Eastern Montana into West-Central North Dakota
Strong deep-layer westerly flow will overspread the northern High Plains during peak heating. Downslope flow trajectories and diurnal heating will produce a deeply mixed boundary layer with surface temperatures in the mid-upper 70s, RH of 20–25%, and sustained west-northwesterly surface winds of 20–25 mph (gusts to 35 mph). Critical highlights reflect the greatest overlap of strong/gusty winds, low RH, and receptive fuels. A north-northwesterly wind shift will accompany frontal passage in the evening; substantial RH recovery expected post-front.
Elevated
Central Plains — Nebraska, South Dakota
Farther south along the lee trough, more substantial downslope warming and drying will yield temperatures in the lower-to-middle 80s and 15–20% RH. Combined with ~15 mph sustained west-southwesterly surface winds and critically dry fuels, elevated to locally critical fire-weather conditions are expected.
