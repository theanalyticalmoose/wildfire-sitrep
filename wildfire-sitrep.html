Weather Discussion

The National Preparedness Level 4 edition of the IMSR did not carry a written Predictive Services narrative; the report instead referenced the standard outlook products: the National 7-Day Significant Fire Potential Outlook, the Storm Prediction Center Fire Weather Outlook, the NWS Fire Weather Hazards Map, and the Weather Prediction Center National Forecast Chart and Short Range Forecast Discussion. For the operative fire-weather picture, see the SPC Day 1 Outlook and NWS Fire Weather Alerts sections below, which were pulled live for this edition. The dominant signal is a hot, dry, breezy southwest flow over the greater Four Corners region with record-high Energy Release Component values across much of the Colorado Rockies.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Issued 1226 AM CDT Thu Jul 02 2026 · Valid 021200Z - 031200Z · Pulled live via browser from SPC (spc.noaa.gov/products/fire_wx/fwdy1).
Critical - Western and southern Colorado, eastern Utah, and northern New Mexico
Population/cities: ~73,295 sq mi, population ~845,150. Larger centers: Pueblo, Grand Junction, Clifton, Canon City, and Montrose, CO.
Reasoning: Southwest sustained winds of 12-20 mph gusting 20-30 mph amid minimum RH of 3-12% across the greater Four Corners region. Poor overnight RH recovery yields extended burn periods; portions of the Critical area may see elevated conditions for nearly 20 hours, including near the Aspen Acres Fire. Large swaths of the Colorado Rockies are near or setting new record-high ERC values.
Elevated - Broader greater Four Corners region surrounding the Critical area (western Colorado, eastern Utah, portions of northern New Mexico)
Population/cities: Population not itemized in the SPC risk table for the Elevated area.
Reasoning: 5-15 hours of at least elevated fire weather conditions expected. ERCs exceed the 90th percentile across the vast majority of the Elevated area amid the same dry, breezy southwest flow.
Localized concerns kept below formal highlight criteria:
  • Northern Great Basin: Isolated dry thunderstorms possible in portions of northeast Nevada into southeast Idaho, but low MUCAPE (<250 J/kg), rising heights and limited mid-level moisture keep confidence low that storms reach receptive fuels. Kept below formal highlight criteria.
