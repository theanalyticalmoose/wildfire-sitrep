Predictive Services Discussion
Strong southwest winds 20-35 with gusts 35-55 mph amid relative humidity of 5-15% will develop across much of New Mexico to the southern High Plains behind the dry line today creating widespread critical to extremely critical conditions. Temperatures are also likely to approach or exceed 100°F in the southern High Plains again today. Snow will fall in the central Rockies to the north of the winds, and strong to severe thunderstorms will develop ahead of the cold front and dry line from North Texas northeast into the Great Lakes. The most widespread severe thunderstorms are expected in eastern Kansas, with several tornadoes possible. Isolated to scattered thunderstorms will develop in the Lower Mississippi Valley to Florida, while well above normal temperatures spread from the Appalachians to the East Coast. Persistently low relative humidity will persist today from the southern Appalachians to the coast, as well. Near to slightly below normal temperatures will continue across much of Alaska today, with isolated to scattered showers.
