Predictive Services Discussion

The southern Intermountain West will continue to see dry and very windy conditions today as the pattern across the West will be slow to change. With poor moisture recovery and another day of rapid drying, fire activity is likely to remain significant for the southern Great Basin, Southwest and Rocky Mountain geographic areas. Lightning holdovers may continue to emerge. Look for relative humidity from 8-15% across the region, with southwesterly winds gusting from 25-50 mph, locally higher in favored downslope terrain. Any extremely critical conditions will be more localized than on Saturday.

The northern Intermountain West will be unseasonably chilly, with record cold high temperatures and some heavy snow in the higher elevations of Idaho, Montana and far northwestern Wyoming. Meanwhile, very hot, dry and breezy conditions will expand across the central and southern High Plains. Alaska will be cool and rainy, with minimal lightning and lower impacts to ongoing fires. Hot and drier conditions followed by isolated afternoon thunderstorms will bring increasing fire risks to the Florida peninsula and portions of the coastal Carolinas.
