Predictive Services Discussion

Widespread elevated to critical conditions will develop across much of the eastern Great Basin, Southwest, and central Rockies today ahead of a strong, dry cold front. Southwest winds 15-30 mph with gusts 30-45 mph will develop amid relative humidity of 5-15%. Isolated dry thunderstorms will develop along the cold front in northern Utah into central and Wyoming and northwest Colorado for potential new ignitions, as well. Strong west winds 20-30 mph with gusts to 45 mph amid humidity of 5-10% will develop behind the front in the Upper Snake River Plain of Idaho, with much lighter winds behind the front in eastern Oregon, southwest Idaho, and northern Nevada. Isolated to scattered showers will develop well behind the front in the Washington Cascades to central Montana, with snow possible at the highest elevations.

In the eastern U.S., breezy south winds 15-20 mph with gusts to 30 mph amid relative humidity near 30% will develop in northern Minnesota creating elevated conditions that received little rainfall last week. Scattered to widespread showers and thunderstorms will develop farther south from Iowa south to central Texas and the Lower Mississippi Valley. A cold front will move through the Mid-Atlantic with isolated to scattered showers and thunderstorms, although rainfall footprints will be small, especially in eastern North Carolina. In Alaska, cool and moist conditions are expected as an Arctic low moves through, with scattered light showers.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Critical — Southwest, Great Basin, and the Central Rockies
Valid 081200Z – 091200Z (Mon Jun 8)
Strong vertical mixing along with a strengthening pressure gradient will result in strong winds to 20-30 mph and RH near 5-15% in the afternoon. Fuels in the area have been persistently dry with poor overnight RH recovery, exacerbating the dry-fuel situation. A Critical fire weather risk has been maintained.
Elevated — Northwest New Mexico (Isolated Dry Thunderstorms)
Valid 081200Z – 091200Z (Mon Jun 8)
Strong diurnal heating along with a compact jet max aloft overlapping a plume of mid-level moisture will likely result in isolated storm development across parts of northwestern New Mexico during the afternoon. A very dry boundary layer and fast storm motions suggest activity will be mainly dry; an Isolated Dry Thunder area has been maintained over the dry fuels.
