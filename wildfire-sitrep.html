Weather Discussion — Predictive Services, NIFC

Widespread elevated to critical conditions due to west to southwesterly winds of 15–30 mph with gusts 30–55 mph will develop across all the High Plains, the Greater Four Corners, the Northern Plains, and Minnesota. Moisture will move north across the central and southern Plains with higher relative humidity, but the moisture will be more limited on the central High Plains and will result in isolated dry thunderstorms in the afternoon.

Very dry conditions will persist in the Southeast with minimum relative humidity of 15–30%, but winds will be lighter today outside of sea breezes that develop off the Gulf and Atlantic coasts. However, breezy west winds are expected in portions of the Mid-Atlantic and the Piedmont. Widespread precipitation will fall across the northern half of the West, with snow falling above 5,000–6,000 feet.

Scattered showers and thunderstorms will also develop in portions of East Texas and Louisiana, with scattered light precipitation in the Northeast from a passing cold front. Below normal temperatures are expected to persist across Alaska today, while breezy trade winds and drier conditions return to Hawai'i.
