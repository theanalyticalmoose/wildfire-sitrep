Predictive Services Discussion

Widespread elevated to critical conditions will persist across portions of the West due to hot and dry conditions as well as the potential for mixed thunderstorms in the southwest. High based thunderstorms are expected across Arizona, New Mexico, and Colorado. Precipitation amounts from these storms will be the highest across eastern New Mexico and portions of southern central Colorado, with the rest of those areas expected to see lower precipitation amounts near or below 0.10 inches. Gusty winds are also expected across the Cascades, central Rockies, Utah, and Southwestern Area in the afternoon and evening. Additionally, hot temperatures are expected across the West from western Washington and Oregon into the Interior valley of California which will combine with low relative humidity levels. Hot temperatures are also expected to span from the Gulf Coast up the Atlantic seaboard which will be followed by the potential for thunderstorms in the afternoon/evening spanning from southern Texas to North Carolina bringing isolated areas of gusty winds.

In Alaska, moderate conditions are expected. Over central eastern Alaska, relative humidity values as low as 30% are expected, with a chance for scattered precipitation to the north and south accompanied by isolated areas of higher winds accompanying scattered storms.
