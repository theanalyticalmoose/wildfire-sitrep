Critical Fire Weather Area
Southwest & Southern/Central Plains — TX Panhandle, E. New Mexico, SW Nebraska, NE Colorado
Wind speeds intensifying from eastern NM into the TX Panhandle by afternoon, driven by surface cyclogenesis over southern KS and northwest OK. With only minimal upper-level clouds and lackluster moisture recovery, RHs will drop to 10% or less with sustained westerly winds of 20–25 mph. The Critical area includes portions of northeast CO and southwest NE, where at least a few hours of Critical conditions are possible before higher RHs arrive late in the day. Isolated pockets of Extremely Critical conditions remain possible in gap-flow corridors of SE Colorado and terrain-influenced areas from eastern CO into the western TX Panhandle — sustained winds of 30 mph may overlap with single-digit RHs in these locales.
Elevated Fire Weather Area
Central Plains — Remainder of E. Colorado, W. Kansas, Most of Nebraska, Portions of High Plains
An Elevated fire weather threat will persist across the central Plains as dry, post-frontal northwesterly winds benefit from downslope enhancement. This threat encompasses portions of the High Plains where the arrival of dry post-frontal air may cause existing wildfires or dormant lightning holdovers from Wednesday to flare up under the sustained dry and breezy regime. Precipitation accumulation overnight will continue to be closely monitored over portions of eastern South Dakota, where elevated wind and RH conditions are likely but fuels are currently expected to become unreceptive.
