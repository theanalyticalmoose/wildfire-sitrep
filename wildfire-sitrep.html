SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Valid 291200Z – 301200Z · Issued 0104 AM CDT Wed Apr 29, 2026
🔶 Elevated Fire Weather Threat
Southwest — Eastern AZ, Central NM / West TX, San Luis Valley
A 50-60 kt mid-level jet at the base of an embedded shortwave within broader west-southwest flow will promote continued dry and breezy conditions. Westerly winds of 15-20 mph and RH of less than 15% amid dry fuels will support an Elevated fire weather threat for portions of eastern AZ into central NM/West TX and the San Luis Valley. Preceding days of fire-effective weather conditions and receptive fuels increase concern. Locally Critical conditions possible on leeward slopes of prominent mountain ranges in southern NM with sustained winds of 20-25 mph and 10% RH.
🔶 Elevated Fire Weather Threat
Southern Georgia and Northern Florida
Beneath the upper level trough, a deepening surface low over the Mid-Atlantic will enhance southwest to westerly surface winds across much of the Southeast. West winds of around 10 mph and RH of 25-35% (locally <25%) are expected over a dry and drought-stressed landscape. This will promote an Elevated fire weather threat across portions of southern GA into northern FL this afternoon. Early morning thunderstorms moving across central AL into far western GA are expected to diminish before affecting the risk area.
