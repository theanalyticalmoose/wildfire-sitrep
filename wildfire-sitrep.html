Predictive Services Discussion

Hot, dry, and breezy conditions are expected across the Inland Northwest into northern California and the western Great Basin today, with downslope winds developing east of the Cascades and Sierra Nevada. Strong winds will extend into the Idaho Panhandle and northwest Montana. Hot, dry weather will also persist across much of California, the Great Plains, and the Mississippi Valley as a broad ridge of high pressure strengthens over the Rockies. Monsoonal moisture circulating beneath the ridge will support scattered to widespread wet and dry thunderstorms across the eastern Great Basin into western Colorado, Wyoming, and Montana. Meanwhile, a cold front will move slowly across the lower Great Lakes into the Northeast, triggering thunderstorms in the Ohio Valley and potentially severe storms into the Mid-Atlantic. Scattered showers and thunderstorms are also expected across portions of the Southeast, including the Florida Panhandle.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

No Risk Areas Forecast. The SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook (valid 181200Z - 191200Z, issued 1250 AM CDT Sat Jul 18 2026) delineates no Critical or Elevated risk areas. Pulled live from spc.noaa.gov.
Forecaster discussion (localized concerns kept below formal highlight criteria):
Along the northwestern periphery of an expansive upper ridge over the West, a midlevel trough will advance eastward across BC and the Northwest during the day. Within the base of the trough, strong midlevel southwesterly flow will overspread WA, OR, northern CA, and northwest NV. Associated downslope flow in the lee of the Cascades and northern Sierra will aid in deep boundary-layer mixing into the strengthening flow aloft. This will yield a broad area of 15 mph sustained west-southwesterly surface winds (with higher gusts) amid 10-15 percent RH. Locally stronger surface winds can be expected in terrain-favored/gap-flow areas through the Cascades. Given dry/receptive fuels across region, elevated fire-weather conditions are expected. Farther east, lee troughing over the northern High Plains will promote 10-15 mph sustained easterly surface winds across parts of southeastern WY during the afternoon. These winds, combined with 15-20 percent RH and dry fuels, will favor elevated fire-weather conditions.
