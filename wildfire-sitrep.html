National Predictive Services Outlook (7-Day Significant Fire Potential Outlook). The NIFC Incident Management Situation Report references the following predictive services products for the national weather discussion: • National Weather Service, Storm Prediction Center: Fire Weather Outlook map and discussion (see the SPC Day 1 Outlook section below). • National Weather Service: Fire Weather Hazards map. • National Weather Service, Weather Prediction Center: National Forecast Chart and Short Range Forecast Discussion. Per the live SPC Day 1 discussion: broad upper troughing over the Interior West promotes Great Basin surface low development, driving dry southwesterly flow across eastern California toward the Four Corners. Deep-layer ascent and northward mid-level moisture transport support high-based thunderstorm development from the northern Great Basin into the Pacific Northwest. The combination of dry, windy conditions and thunderstorms over receptive fuels supports efficient wildfire ignition and spread across a sizeable portion of the Interior West today.

Critical — Portions of far northern California into extreme southern Oregon and northwestern Nevada
Affected: Northern Sierra and adjacent terrain within the broader dry-thunderstorm zone (pop ~783,527 incl. Reno, Sparks, Carson City and Sun Valley, NV and Ashland, OR)

Reasoning: Ahead of a developing Great Basin surface low, afternoon mixing of a deep boundary layer brings 15-20 mph sustained southwesterly winds with 10-15% RH over dry fuels.
