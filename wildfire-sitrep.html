SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook — Valid May 17 1200Z – May 18 1200Z
⚠ EXTREMELY CRITICAL FIRE WEATHER AREA
Extreme eastern New Mexico · Extreme southeastern Colorado · Far southwestern Kansas · TX and OK Panhandles

While sustained surface winds may fall just shy of typical Extremely Critical criteria, the combination of very dry conditions (5–15% RH), 25–30 mph sustained west-southwesterly surface winds, heavy fuel loading exceeding the 95th percentile, and potential pre-existing holdovers from earlier fire starts creates a volatile landscape with dangerous rapid fire-spread potential. Downward mixing of stronger flow aloft in the post-dryline environment is the primary driver.
🔴 CRITICAL FIRE WEATHER AREA
Much of New Mexico into the Southern High Plains

A dryline sharpening along the TX Panhandle/western Oklahoma border through the afternoon will drive a very dry boundary layer mixing to at least 600 mb. Critical highlights maintained for the broad zone from central NM to the immediate dryline vicinity where 25–30 mph winds and 5–15% RH are expected. Dangerous fire weather conditions across the southern High Plains today.
🔴 CRITICAL FIRE WEATHER AREA
Sacramento Valley, California

Deep-layer north-northwesterly flow on the backside of an upper trough will bring a mid-level jet streak over California. Downslope flow from higher terrain will support surface winds exceeding 25 mph in spots as RH dips to the 15–20% range. Given dry fuels, Critical highlights introduced to the Sacramento Valley region.
🟠 ELEVATED FIRE WEATHER AREA
San Joaquin Valley, California

Broader Elevated highlights extend into the San Joaquin Valley where 15+ mph sustained northerly winds will overlap with 15–20% RH conditions.
