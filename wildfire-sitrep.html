Predictive Services Weather Discussion

A pair of low pressure systems will progress across the central and eastern portions of the U.S. Saturday bringing showers & thunderstorms. One of the low pressure systems will spread from the South and Southeast U.S. northeastward to the East Coast states, eastern Great Lakes and southern New England. Significant rainfall amounts in the half inch to 2.5 inch range are expected across portions of the Gulf Coast states, Georgia and the Carolinas northward to New York, eastern Indiana, Ohio, Michigan, and New Jersey. Meanwhile, dry conditions persist further north across New England. The second low pressure system will drop more scattered and mostly lesser tenth to 1 inch amounts across Texas and the Southern & Central Plains north to Iowa, Minnesota & North Dakota. Mostly dry conditions are likely across the Western U.S. Dry and locally breezy conditions are likely across much of the Southwest & Great Basin with some valley minimum relative humidities in the single digits to teens & locally breezy conditions. Weak low pressure & instability is likely to lead to isolated mixed wet & dry afternoon to early evening thunderstorms across portions of Northern California, Southern Oregon, and Nevada.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Valid: 231200Z – 241200Z  ·  Issued 1251 AM CDT Sat May 23, 2026

No Critical or Elevated Areas
Upper-level ridging will gradually build across much of the western CONUS through the period. Warming temperatures will support RH reductions to 10–20% across much of the Southwest and portions of the Great Basin this afternoon. Weak surface pressure gradient and generally light surface winds will largely temper broad fire weather concerns. Sustained westerly/southwesterly winds of 10–15 mph may promote locally elevated fire weather concerns in favored gap/terrain areas. Mid-level moisture may support weak buoyancy and isolated high-based convection over higher terrain across portions of the western Great Basin into far northeastern California and southern Oregon this afternoon. Generally limited lightning potential and sub-critical fuels should temper the overall lightning ignition threat.
