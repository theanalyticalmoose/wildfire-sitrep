SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Valid 12/1200Z – 13/1200Z (Fri Jun 12, 2026)

SPC delineated no formal Critical or Extremely Critical risk areas on the Day 1 outlook map today. The forecast discussion highlights two regions of elevated (locally critical) fire-weather conditions:

Elevated — Eastern Great Basin into the Central Rockies / Adjacent High Plains
Diurnal heating of a dry antecedent air mass with poor overnight recoveries will drive deep boundary-layer mixing, with RH in the single digits to lower/middle teens and 15-20 mph sustained westerly winds (locally higher over terrain-favored areas). With critically dry fuels (90th-95th percentile ERCs), elevated to locally critical fire-weather conditions are expected.
Elevated — Snake River Plain (southern Idaho)
Strengthening midlevel westerly flow in the base of the trough will overspread a deeply mixed boundary layer, producing 10-15 percent RH and 15-20 mph sustained westerly surface winds atop dry, receptive fuels — promoting elevated fire-weather conditions during the afternoon.
