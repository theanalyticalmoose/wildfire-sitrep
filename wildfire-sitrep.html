SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

Valid: Wed May 13, 2026 1200Z – Thu May 14, 2026 1200Z

🔴 CRITICAL — Northeastern Montana into Far Northwestern North Dakota
Northeastern MT and far western ND
Deepening surface troughing across the southern Canadian Prairies into central MT will usher in stronger southerly to southeasterly winds of 15–20 mph along with RH reductions to 20% or below, atop receptive fuels. Critical fire weather conditions are expected where an alignment of RH ≤20% and winds of 20–25 mph converge across northeastern MT and far western ND. Increasing mid-level moisture ahead of the upper trough and arrival of a Pacific cold front will promote isolated to scattered showers and initially high-based thunderstorms across much of northwestern MT.
⚠ ELEVATED — Southwest, Upper Snake River Plain & Colorado River Basin
Great Basin, Colorado River Basin, portions of central WY
A sharp upper trough and associated mid-level jet shifts over ID into western MT, deepening surface troughing across the northern Great Basin. Dry and breezy southwesterly flow of 15–25 mph (localized 30 mph in the eastern Great Basin) and RH of 10–15% will align with drying fuels. Isolated high-based showers and thunderstorms possible over higher terrain of western NM, far eastern AZ, central UT, and southwest WY. Mixed curing fuels result in expansive Elevated highlights and a broader Isolated Dry Thunderstorm risk area.
⚠ ELEVATED — Northern Montana (Broad Area)
Central MT southerly wind corridor
Broad Elevated fire weather concerns across northern MT driven by southerly to southeasterly winds of 15–20 mph and RH reductions to 20–30%, outside the Critical highlight area.
