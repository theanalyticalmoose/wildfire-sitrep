Predictive Services Weather Discussion
A closed, cold upper-level low pressure system over California will bring well below normal temperatures and showers across California, southern Oregon, and western Nevada. Some high elevation snow is also likely in the Sierras, mainly above 8,000-foot elevations. On the northeast side of this low, showers and scattered thunderstorms are expected across the Pacific Northwest, into the northwestern Great Basin, and across Montana. Wetting precipitation amounts of a quarter of an inch up to 1.5 inches are likely across portions of central & southern Oregon, northern & central California, and part of far western Nevada. Well above normal temperatures are likely across the northern tier of the lower 48 states from Washington across the central & northern Rockies, across the Northern Plains, and into the Upper Midwest. Breezy to windy and dry conditions are likely across the Four Corners region. Remnants of a stalled front will bring some showers & thunderstorms along & near the Front Range of the Rockies, into portions of the central and southern Plains and into the Southeast to include Florida. Some half inch to 1.5-inch amounts are likely from Kansas to Arkansas, along the Gulf Coast, across Florida, and in northeastern North Carolina. Some light showers are expected across the Northeast as a cooler air mass moves in from Canada.

Arizona, Far Western New Mexico, Eastern Great Basin, Colorado Western Slope
An upper-level low anchored over the Sierra Nevada and corresponding surface low over western ID will bolster dry and breezy southerly flow across portions of the Great Basin and Southwest this afternoon. South winds of 15–25 mph, RH values of 15–20% (as low as 10% in valleys), and dry/cured fuels in lower elevations will support Elevated to locally critical fire weather concerns.
⚠ Elevated — Northeastern Montana
Northeastern Montana
Surface troughing extending across the northern High Plains into the Northern Rockies and surface high pressure over the Great Lakes region will reinforce dry southeasterly flow atop recently receptive fuels in northeastern MT. Elevated fire weather conditions expected as daytime relative humidity declines to around 20% and southeasterly sustained winds increase to 15–25 mph.
