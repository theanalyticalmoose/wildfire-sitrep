Predictive Services Discussion

Scattered showers and a mix of wet and dry thunderstorms will spread across the Southwest and Four Corners, with the greatest coverage over Utah and the central Great Basin. Heavy rainfall and flash flooding are possible across much of Arizona and southwestern New Mexico. Monsoonal moisture will expand westward into western Nevada and the Sierra Nevada, where drier thunderstorms and gusty outflow winds are more likely. Isolated thunderstorms are also possible across northeastern California and southern Oregon. Hot, dry conditions will persist across the Pacific Northwest and Great Plains, where relative humidity will fall to 10–15% and temperatures will remain well above normal. Winds across Central North and South Dakota are likely to gust up to 40 mph. Breezy northwest winds will continue from the northern Plains into the Great Lakes, accompanied by thunderstorms, some of which may become severe. Across the Southeast, widespread rainfall will continue along Florida's Gulf Coast, with scattered showers and thunderstorms elsewhere across the region. Far southern Florida will remain comparatively dry and breezy, while deep south Texas continues a dry pattern with little or no rainfall expected.

SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook

No Risk Areas Forecast. The SPC Day 1 Fire Weather Outlook (valid 201200Z - 211200Z, issued 0148 AM CDT Mon Jul 20 2026) delineates no Critical or Elevated risk areas. Pulled live from spc.noaa.gov.
Forecaster discussion (localized concerns kept below formal highlight criteria):
Along the northwestern periphery of an expansive upper ridge over the West, a low-amplitude midlevel disturbance and accompanying influx of midlevel moisture will overspread parts of the Northwest today. While this should support isolated high-based showers, weak buoyancy and warm EL temperatures should limit thunderstorm potential. With that said, an isolated storm or two cannot be entirely ruled out over the Cascades in OR. Elsewhere, a limited overlap of dry/windy conditions atop receptive fuels will reduce fire-weather concerns across the CONUS.
